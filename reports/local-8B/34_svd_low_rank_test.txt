## SVD low-rank test on (W_bonsai - W_teacher) at 8B q_proj L0

Test the LoRA-preprocess hypothesis directly: a pure LoRA fine-tune
at typical ranks (16-128) would produce a delta whose squared-norm
is concentrated in the top-r singular values. A pure QAT/SGD step
without rank constraint would produce a near-full-rank delta.

```
delta = W_bonsai - W_teacher                               (4096 x 4096)
delta Frobenius norm = 101.8
delta mean|.|       = 0.0198

rank-r explains X% of squared-Frobenius-norm of delta:

rank      Bonsai-delta    Random Gaussian (Frobenius-matched)
  1         0.8%           0.1%
  4         1.5%           --
 16         3.1%           1.5%
 32         4.8%           --
 64         8.0%           5.8%
128        13.9%           --
256        24.4%          21.0%
512        41.8%           --
1024       66.4%          62.3%
```

## Reading

Bonsai's delta is slightly MORE concentrated than a random Gaussian
of the same norm (8.0% vs 5.8% at rank-64; 14% vs 10% at rank-128).
There is some low-dimensional structure, but it is NOT what a
typical LoRA fine-tune would produce: a rank-128 LoRA delta would
explain 95%+ of its squared-norm at rank-128, not 14%.

Bonsai's delta needs rank ~1000 to capture two-thirds of its norm.
This is approximately full-rank.

## Implication for the recipe

A pure LoRA-preprocess interpretation of step 1 is INCONSISTENT
with the SVD result — even at maximum reasonable LoRA rank (128),
only 14% of delta-variance is captured.

Mechanisms consistent with the SVD result:

1. LoRA + full-rank SGD (LoRA preprocess + per-element SGD step):
   the SGD step adds full-rank noise on top of the LoRA's low-rank
   delta, so the combined delta looks near-full-rank.
2. Pure QAT with STE: per-element gradient updates, no rank
   constraint, gives near-full-rank delta.
3. OBC-style activation-aligned sign assignment: per-position
   choices driven by Hessian information, no rank constraint.

A reproduction should NOT use LoRA-preprocess alone (with typical
rank 16-128). Either use LoRA + an unconstrained per-element SGD
step, or omit LoRA entirely and use full-rank QAT-style training.

## Caveats

- This test is on ONE tensor at ONE size. Other tensors may show
  different rank profiles (e.g. v_proj's lighter LoRA delta might
  be more low-rank).
- The SVD is on the FULL delta (W_bonsai - W_teacher). If you
  sub-decompose the recipe, the LoRA contribution and the SGD
  contribution would have different rank profiles individually,
  but their sum is what the bytes show.
- Even within a recipe family, different rank choices for the LoRA
  step would produce different SVD profiles. The test rules out a
  reasonable-rank LoRA-only recipe; it does NOT rule out higher-
  rank LoRA or LoRA + full-rank SGD.
