## Pattern (revising the earlier "mid-stack only" claim)

q_norm and k_norm: round-trip-tight (≤ 0.6x BF16 ULP) at every layer.
  Confirmed across all 36 layers. **These are frozen at base values.**

input_layernorm: a structured depth profile, NOT a flat "mid-stack
  only" shape. Three regimes:
  - Layer 0: huge excess (53x ULP) — most disturbed of all.
  - Layers 1-13: moderate excess (1.4-19x ULP).
  - Layers 14-28: high steady excess (3-12x ULP), peak 11.6x at L21.
  - Layers 29-35: gradually settles to round-trip-tight (0.3-1.7x).
  L35 is round-trip-tight (within 0x of ULP).

post_attn_layernorm: ≤4x ULP at every layer; never strongly disturbed.
  L35 has the largest excess (0.9x — barely above tight).

## Implications

- The frozen-vs-trained split is even sharper than I'd written: q/k_norm
  are EVERYWHERE frozen; layer norms are differentially trained, with
  input_ln getting the bulk of the work and post_attn_ln barely touching.

- Layer 0's input_layernorm is the single most-modified norm tensor in
  the whole network. That's a strong signal — whatever the technique
  needed to do, it concentrated effort on layer 0's input rescaling.

- Layer 35's input_ln is at 0.0x excess — essentially identical to base
  to the bit. The technique didn't update it.

This is a richer pattern than fits H_a/H_b/H_c straightforwardly. It is
consistent with H_d (forward-sequential) — the layer-0 input_ln
update could be a global activation calibration step at the start of
the sequential procedure.
