## L1-3 MLP teacher-structure confound: the spike is partially
   intrinsic to Qwen3-8B's teacher, not just a Bonsai recipe choice

This report documents a 4th potential confound on the "L1-3 MLP
disturbance spike" interpretation, discovered late in the session.

## How it was found

A LoRA-rank simulation was run to quantify how a small-rank LoRA
perturbation would affect over-dispersion at L1 ffn_gate. When the
simulation was run with Gaussian rank-r LoRA at L1 teacher
calibrated to L1's flip rate (0.34), the result was:

```
r=1: over-dispersion 14.40
r=2: 13.35
r=4: 13.23
r=8: 12.95
r=16: 12.89
r=32: 12.86
r=64: 12.83
r=128: 12.82
```

All ranks gave over-dispersion 12-14 — close to Bonsai's actual
10.27 at L1 ffn_gate. But that's strange: rank-128 LoRA should
behave like full-rank Gaussian noise, which the original Gaussian-
control simulation in `37_*` showed gives over-dispersion ~1.0 at
q L0.

Cross-check at L0 teacher with the same flip rate (0.34) showed:
```
r=1: 8.83
r=2: 4.71
r=4: 2.60
r=8: 1.77
r=16: 1.40
r=32: 1.23
r=64: 1.15
r=128: 1.11
```

At L0 teacher, rank-128 gives Binomial-like over-dispersion (1.11) —
matching the q L0 control. **At L1 teacher, rank-128 gives 12.82
instead of the expected ~1.1.** Something about L1 teacher's
weight distribution produces high over-dispersion REGARDLESS of
the perturbation's rank structure.

## What's unusual about Qwen3-8B's L1-3 ffn_gate teacher weights

Per-block statistics of `|w_teacher|` distribution at 8B:

```
location          block_meanabs_CV    within_block_CV_mean    within_block_CV_std
L0  gate                0.114                0.763                  0.054
L1  gate                1.007                1.674                  1.644
L2  gate                1.128                1.805                  1.668
L3  gate                0.834                1.792                  1.950
L9  gate                0.181                0.762                  0.064
L18 gate                0.165                0.777                  0.091
L27 gate                0.123                0.762                  0.067
L35 gate                0.163                0.759                  0.054

L1  up                  0.987                0.848                  0.150
L2  up                  1.130                0.973                  0.462
L3  up                  0.808                0.826                  0.139
(elsewhere up CV is ~0.76 with std ~0.06)

L1-35 q_proj            0.17-0.27            ~0.77                  ~0.06
```

L1-L3 ffn_gate has **~10× higher cross-block CV of mean-|w| (1.0
vs 0.1)** and **~30× higher within-block CV variability (1.7 vs
0.05)** than other depths. ffn_up shows a milder version of the
same pattern at L1-3 (blk_cv ~1.0 vs baseline ~0.1; within-block
CV slightly elevated). q_proj is uniform across all depths.

This is a property of **Qwen3-8B's pretrained weights** — not a
Bonsai recipe choice. It's intrinsic to the teacher.

## Why this produces high over-dispersion under any perturbation

If different blocks within a tensor have very different |w|
distributions (some blocks have many near-zero positions, others
have all moderately-sized positions), then under any sigma-bounded
perturbation:
- Near-zero-heavy blocks: most positions flip (rate near 0.5)
- Heavy blocks: few positions flip (rate near 0)
- Mixed blocks: intermediate rate

So the per-block flip count varies enormously across blocks
**because the teacher's per-block magnitude distribution is
heterogeneous**, not because the perturbation has block-coherent
structure.

The simulation confirms this: at L1 teacher, even random Gaussian
noise (rank-128 LoRA ≈ random Gaussian) produces over-dispersion
of ~13. The teacher structure dominates the over-dispersion
contribution.

## Revised interpretation of the L1-3 over-dispersion spike

**The L1-3 spike is partially attributable to Qwen3-8B's teacher
structure**, NOT solely to Bonsai's recipe applying a special
mechanism at L1-3.

A simpler decomposition:

- **Intrinsic-teacher contribution at L1-3 gate**: ~10-14× over-
  dispersion under uniform Gaussian noise calibrated to the
  observed flip rate.
- **Bonsai's actual over-dispersion at L1-3 gate**: 10.27.

So Bonsai's L1-3 MLP "spike" might be explained almost entirely by
the teacher's intrinsic block-heterogeneity, with Bonsai's recipe
just producing roughly Gaussian-calibrated flips on top.

## What this changes about the previous L1-3 reports

`38_*`, `40_*` documented the over-dispersion spike. **The factual
measurement is correct**, but the *interpretation* —
"Bonsai's recipe has a stronger block-coherent decision step at
L1-3" — is now substantially weakened.

`41_*` documented that L1-3 has higher rank concentration (rank-128
~10-12% vs baseline 7.5-8%). **This finding is unaffected.** The
rank-concentration uptick IS a property of the (W_bonsai - W_teacher)
delta, independent of the teacher's structure.

`42_*` documented that the spike is 8B-specific (not at 1.7B).
**This finding is now interpretable through the new lens**: maybe
Qwen3-1.7B's L1-3 ffn_gate teacher is NOT as heterogeneous as
Qwen3-8B's, so the same recipe applied to both teachers produces
different over-dispersion at L1-3. Direct test pending (1.7B base
needs to be re-fetched).

`43_*` documented the spike is MLP-specific (no spike at attn).
**This finding is consistent with the new lens**: q_proj teacher
weights are uniform across all depths (blk_cv ~0.2 throughout) so
no intrinsic over-dispersion contribution at L1-3 attention.

`44_*`'s within-block-ordering hypothesis: was already weakened by
verifier-3 corrections. Now further weakened — the asymmetric
MLP-vs-attention pattern at L1-3 might be substantially explained
by teacher structure alone (gate teacher is heterogeneous, q
teacher is uniform), not by within-block ordering.

## Recipe constraints, refined

What the bytes still attest:
- The recipe produces approximately full-rank delta everywhere
  (`34_*`, `39_*`, `41_*`, `43_*`).
- The recipe produces flip rates that vary by (depth, type)
  (`40_*`).
- The recipe's L1-3 MLP flip rate (0.32-0.38) is higher than the
  baseline (0.24-0.28) — this is a real recipe-driven elevation,
  NOT a teacher artifact.
- The recipe's per-block scale ratios are 1.5-2.4× teacher
  mean(|w|) (`27_*`).
- The recipe's per-block over-dispersion at L1-3 MLP is 10-13,
  but most of this might be explained by teacher block-magnitude
  heterogeneity rather than by recipe block-coherence.

What's WEAKER now:
- The earlier framing that "L1-3 MLP receives extra block-coherent
  recipe decisions" is partly an artifact of teacher structure.
- The within-block-ordering interpretation is further weakened.

## Process note

This is the FOURTH confounder caught in this session. The pattern
holds: every "remarkable" finding deserves a "what would random
noise produce here?" control. The verifier sub-agent caught three;
the fourth was found by extending an existing analysis (the LoRA-
rank simulation) with a comparison across layers.

The repo's discipline of reporting force-by-data separately from
inference is what makes this kind of correction possible without
having to retract the underlying measurements.
