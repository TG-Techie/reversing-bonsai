## Per-block flip-count over-dispersion at 8B q_proj L0

For each 128-block in the tensor, count flips (positions where
sign(W_bonsai) != sign(W_teacher)). Bin blocks by teacher mean(|w|)
into deciles. Compare observed flip-count variance to the Binomial
expectation `128 * p * (1-p)` (the variance under per-element
independent flipping at rate p).


## Reading

- Over-dispersion ratio = observed-variance / Binomial-expected-
  variance.
- Ratio = 1.0: per-element independent flips (Binomial).
- Ratio >> 1: some blocks flip much more, others much less than
  expected — per-block correlation.
- Ratio < 1: regularised / anti-correlated (e.g., enforced quotas).

The observed ratios are 1.2-3.4 — over-dispersed in every decile.
Especially at extremes (d1=2.74, d10=3.39).

**Per-element independent flipping is RULED OUT.** The flip pattern
has block-level correlation: the decision to flip particular
positions within a block is not independent of decisions for other
positions in the same block.

## What this is consistent with

- OBC-style activation-aware optimisation, where each block's signs
  are decided jointly to minimise layer-output error. Block-as-unit
  decisions naturally produce over-dispersion in flip counts.
- A structured LoRA delta with low rank r << 128 — rank-r delta
  affects multiple positions per block coherently, so flips
  correlate within blocks.
- A loss function that rewards block-level coherence (e.g. some
  group lasso variants).

## What this rules out

- Pure i.i.d. Gaussian noise on individual weights with no block
  structure. That would give Binomial flip counts (overdisp ~1.0),
  not the 1.2-3.4 we observe.
- Any per-element-uncorrelated stochastic perturbation.

## Recipe constraint

A reproduction's sign-flip mechanism must produce **block-level
correlated flips**. Pure independent per-element noise on the teacher
will not reproduce the over-dispersion. Mechanisms that DO produce
block-correlated flips include:

1. OBC-style sequential block-wise optimisation against activation
   outputs.
2. Low-rank LoRA structure (rank r affects many block positions
   coherently).
3. Group-aware regularisation in the SGD step.

The bytes don't discriminate among (1)-(3); they only rule out the
i.i.d. baseline.

## Caveats

- One tensor (8B q_proj L0). Per-tensor over-dispersion may vary;
  v_proj might be MORE coherent (consistent with v's lighter
  perturbation), MLP might be LESS coherent.
- Over-dispersion test is sensitive to magnitude-binning. The
  decile choice here uses the Bonsai-8B q_proj L0 distribution;
  finer or coarser bins might shift the numbers slightly but not
  the qualitative finding.
