## Cross-tensor over-dispersion: where is block-level coupling
   strong vs weak?

Refines the within-block flip-correlation finding from `37_*` (q L0,
overdispersion 1.2-3.4 in deciles, 2.20 overall). That single
tensor showed strong over-dispersion. This experiment scans
3 depths (L0, L18, L35) × 7 projection types at 8B to ask: is the
block-level coupling uniform, or does it vary by (depth, type)?

## Method

Per tensor: take all 128-blocks, count flips (positions where
sign(W_bonsai) != sign(W_teacher), excluding teacher zeros — restrict
to blocks with no teacher zeros, which is nearly all of them).
Compute observed variance of per-block flip-count, compare to the
Binomial-expected `128 * p * (1-p)`. Ratio > 1 = block-correlated;
ratio = 1 = per-element independent.

## Reading

Over-dispersion is NOT uniform. Two clear patterns emerge:

1. **By projection type at L0**: attn_q is most over-dispersed (2.20).
   attn_v, ffn_gate, ffn_up at L0 are essentially Binomial
   (1.07-1.17). attn_k, attn_output, ffn_down sit in between
   (1.22-1.61).

2. **Depth pattern**: For MLP types (gate, up), over-dispersion is
   essentially Binomial at L0 (~1.07-1.12) and grows monotonically
   to >2.0 at L35. For attention types (q, k, v, o), over-dispersion
   is already non-trivial at L0 and rises modestly with depth.
   ffn_down is the exception — flat across depth.

## What this constrains

- **The block-level coupling mechanism is depth- and type-dependent.**
  Whatever produces over-dispersion in q_proj at every depth and in
  late MLP gate/up does NOT produce it in v_proj L0 or early MLP
  gate/up. The same recipe step cannot apply uniformly.

- **At early layers and on v/gate/up, per-element independent
  flipping is consistent with the bytes**. Block coupling is not
  required there. But attention q/k/o always shows some coupling.

- **Late layers (L35) have stronger block coupling across all types**
  except ffn_down. This is consistent with deeper layers receiving
  a stronger or more block-structured perturbation (e.g., a low-rank
  delta with rank that grows with depth, or sequential-quantisation
  error that compounds layerwise).

## What this rules in / rules out

- A pure-i.i.d.-Gaussian noise model is RULED OUT for q_proj at all
  depths (consistent with `37_*`), for late MLP, and for o_proj at
  L18/L35.
- For v_proj L0/L18 and early MLP gate/up at L0, the i.i.d. model
  is CONSISTENT with the bytes (over-dispersion within statistical
  noise of 1.0).
- A uniform block-level mechanism (same low-rank-r LoRA delta
  applied identically to all tensors) is RULED OUT — that would
  give roughly uniform over-dispersion. The actual technique
  produces block-coupled flips at some (layer, type) pairs and not
  others.

## Mechanism families consistent with this depth/type pattern

Three candidates, listed in order of how naturally they produce the
observed pattern:

1. **OBC-style sequential per-block activation-aware quantisation**:
   per-block decisions explicitly depend on prior blocks' activation
   reconstruction. Late layers see error compounded from earlier
   layers; tensors more sensitive to activations show more
   block-structured fits. Naturally produces depth-growing
   over-dispersion and tensor-type variation.

2. **A low-rank LoRA delta whose rank or magnitude grows with depth**
   for some tensor types but not others. Pure low-rank deltas
   produce block-coupled flips by construction.

3. **A loss function that includes block-coherence regularisation
   that fires more strongly at deep layers** — possible but ad-hoc;
   the 1-bit Bonsai whitepaper's "principled framework" claim makes
   this less likely.

The bytes don't discriminate (1) from (2). Both produce
block-coupled flips with depth/type variation.

## Caveats

- The Binomial baseline assumes independent per-element flipping at
  the empirical mean rate p. If teacher signs within a block are
  themselves correlated (e.g., due to low-rank teacher structure),
  the baseline shifts. We checked at q L0 (`37_*`) where teacher
  per-block sign distribution looks well-mixed; not separately
  re-checked here.
- 3 depths sampled (L0, L18, L35); the depth shape between is not
  measured here.

## Updated implication for `RECIPE_HINTS.md`

The block-correlation finding should be qualified:
"Block-level flip correlation is present in some tensors and depths
but not others. Strongest in deep MLP gate/up and in attention q
across all depths; absent or weak in early MLP and v_proj at early
depths." A reproduction must reproduce this pattern, not just
average over-dispersion.
