## Per-row amplitude inheritance from teacher: Bonsai vs Qwen3-8B-base

For each (layer, projection_type) at 8B, compute:
- `teacher_row_means`: per-row mean(|w_teacher|) — the teacher's per-output-channel amplitude
- `bonsai_row_means`: per-row mean(deployed s_g) — Bonsai's per-output-channel deployed scale

Correlate them across rows. High correlation means the technique
amplifies the same rows the teacher had loud. Low correlation means
the technique picks different rows to amplify.

## Reading

For attention projections (q/k/v/o) and `down_proj`, Bonsai's per-row
amplitude is strongly aligned with the teacher's per-row amplitude.
The technique inherits which output channels are loud. So the
"top-1% block" row-clustering finding from `24_*` is, for these
projections, **just a reflection of the teacher's natural row
amplitudes**, not an independent recipe choice.

For MLP `gate_proj` and `up_proj`, Bonsai's per-row amplitude is
roughly half-correlated with teacher's. The technique is making
independent decisions about which gate/up output channels to
amplify, especially at late layers (L35 gate Pearson +0.21, L35 up
Pearson -0.22). This is consistent with the disturbance-axis
finding from `23_*`: MLP gate/up is where Bonsai deviates most from
teacher in both signs and scales.

## Recipe constraint

A reproduction targeting Bonsai-style behaviour:

1. For attention + `down_proj`: per-block scales should track the
   teacher's per-row magnitude profile (Pearson 0.7-0.86). A
   reasonable initialisation for these projections is
   `s_g_init = mean(|w_teacher|_per_block)` — close to the formula.
2. For MLP `gate_proj` and `up_proj`: per-block scale assignment is
   NOT teacher-row-following. Use a calibration-data-driven
   optimisation that's allowed to pick different rows to amplify
   than the teacher had loud. PTQ1.61's SGD-α with output-cosine
   objective is a published candidate.

The asymmetry (attention follows teacher; MLP gate/up doesn't) is a
hard recipe-relevant signature. None of the 9 prior-art papers
digested in `PRIOR_ART_VERDICT_MATRIX.md` predict this asymmetry.
