## Reading

The implied LoRA delta std varies by projection type. v_proj and
down_proj receive lighter LoRA delta (sigma/mean ratio ~0.72-0.87)
than q/k/o/gate/up (ratio ~1.05-1.24).

This explains the per-projection sign-agreement ordering observed
in 20_*: v (0.79) and down (0.78) have the highest sign-agreement
with teacher because their LoRA preprocess is lighter; gate/up
have lower agreement because heavier LoRA disturbance.

## Recipe constraint

A reproduction's LoRA configuration should NOT apply uniform
strength across all linear module types. Recommended scaling:

```
  v_proj, down_proj:  ~70-90% of the standard LoRA strength
  q/k/o/gate/up:      standard LoRA strength
```

The sigma/teacher_mean ratio at 8B for "standard" projections is
~1.1-1.24. So the LoRA delta std should be 1.1-1.24 times the
teacher's mean(|w|) on those tensors, and 0.7-0.9 times for v/down.

## Cross-size check (quick)

The 1.7B q L0 fit gave sigma=0.04 with teacher_mean=0.027, ratio=1.5.
The 8B q L0 fit gives sigma=0.025 with teacher_mean=0.020, ratio=1.24.

At 1.7B the ratio is higher (heavier relative LoRA), at 8B lower.
Cross-size: bigger Bonsai = lighter LoRA preprocess relatively.

## Caveats

- Tested at L0 only. Per-layer sigma may vary; the early-MLP-dip
  finding suggests gate/up might have heavier sigma at L1-3.
  **Refinement (`45_*`)**: at L1-L3 the elevated flip rate may be
  partly explained by the teacher's intrinsic block-magnitude
  heterogeneity rather than a heavier recipe-applied sigma. The
  L1-L3 sign-agreement dip is not necessarily a "more LoRA disturbance
  at those layers" signal — could be the teacher's many near-zero
  positions flipping at near-random rate. The per-kind sigma ranking
  in the table above (measured at L0) is unaffected by this
  refinement.
- Gaussian noise model is a simplification; real LoRA is low-rank.
- The sigma fit is a 1-parameter model matched to overall
  sign-agreement only; not all aspects of Bonsai's bytes are
  captured by the fit. Specifically, the over-dispersion findings
  in `37_*`-`45_*` show the actual flip pattern has block-level
  structure not captured by i.i.d. Gaussian noise.
