## Reading

embed_tokens: r²=1.000, slope=1, intercept=0, median ratio = 1.0, max
diff 1e-4. So Bonsai's embed s_g IS exactly mean(σ_bonsai · w_base) per
block. Combined with the earlier finding that the SIGNS of Bonsai.embed
match Qwen3-base byte-for-byte (99.94% sign agreement), the embed is
fully described by: take Qwen3-base.embed, replace each weight with
sign(w) * mean(|w|_per_block). That's the formula.

lm_head: r²=0.264 — fit is mediocre. Median ratio of stored s_g to
RMSE-optimal value = 1.17 — so Bonsai's lm_head scales are slightly
larger than RMSE-optimal vs base.

q_proj/v_proj/mlp.gate: r² = 0.34-0.60, median ratio 1.9-2.3.
Bonsai's matrix-heavy scales are roughly *2× the RMSE-optimal value*
for the chosen signs onto base. So: even given σ_bonsai (whatever
optimisation chose those signs), the deployed scales are NOT
minimising distance to Qwen3-base. They're amplified by ~2× past
that minimum.

What this rules out:
- "Scale = mean(|w_base|_g) directly" (we already saw 0% scale match).
- "Scale = RMSE-optimal projection of σ_bonsai onto w_base" (median
  ratio is 1.9-2.3, not 1.0).

What's still consistent:
- Scale was learned to minimise *some other objective* — distillation
  loss on activations, KL on output logits, etc. — where preserving
  activation magnitude past the matmul matters more than minimising
  ‖weight - teacher_weight‖.
- Scale was set to maintain output norms across the layer post-quant
  (a 'magnitude calibration' step rather than a per-weight fit).

Recipe constraint for a reproduction:
- Pure formula init for embed (and probably q_norm/k_norm).
- Matrix-heavy: any reproduction that fits scales by L2 distance to
  the base weights will land too quiet by ~2x. The technique used a
  loss that *valued amplification* — likely activation-output-driven.
