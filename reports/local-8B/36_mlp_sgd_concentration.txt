## SGD-alpha step's work concentration at MLP (8B)

Same per-row LoRA-vs-Bonsai test as the 1.7B q_proj L0 baseline
(reports/local-1.7B/32_*), repeated at MLP gate / up across depths
at 8B. LoRA simulation uses sigma = 1.25 * teacher_mean (the 8B
typical ratio from 33_*).

```
tensor              overall match    rows >60%   rows <20%   col CV
L0  mlp.gate         0.4620            29%        13%         0.027
L18 mlp.gate         0.0503             2%        93%         0.077
L35 mlp.gate         0.1372            10%        77%         0.029
L35 mlp.up           0.2175            17%        65%         0.020
```

Compare to q_proj baseline (1.7B q L0 from 32_*):
- overall match 0.435, rows >60% 38%, rows <20% 30%, col CV 0.008.

## Reading

L0 mlp.gate behaves similarly to the q_proj baseline (~45% overall
match). But at deeper MLP gate (L18, L35) and at L35 mlp.up, the
LoRA-only simulation gets very few blocks right: only 5% match at
L18 mlp.gate, with 93% of rows having <20% of their blocks within
10% of Bonsai's deployed scale.

The SGD-alpha step does the overwhelming majority of corrective
work at deep MLP gate and up. In contrast, attention projections
and early-layer MLP (L0) are mostly handled by LoRA + naive Q1_0
alone.

Column CV is uniformly small (<0.08) at every tensor sampled. The
unmatched-vs-matched distinction is per-row, not per-column.

## Recipe constraint

A reproduction's SGD-alpha step's compute budget should be
ALLOCATED ASYMMETRICALLY:

- Attention layers and L0 MLP: most blocks converge with LoRA + a
  handful of SGD steps.
- Mid-stack and deep MLP gate/up: most blocks need substantial SGD
  refinement to land at Bonsai-style scales. Allocate proportionally
  more compute here.

This pattern matches the per-row Pearson finding (26_*): at MLP
gate/up, deep layers' deployed alpha deviates most from teacher's
mean(|w|). The two findings together identify deep MLP gate/up as
the recipe's HOTSPOT — where the technique invests the most compute.

A reproduction targeting Bonsai-style behaviour with limited SGD
budget should prioritise deep MLP gate/up.

## Caveats

- The "LoRA simulation" uses Gaussian noise with sigma matched to
  overall sign-agreement, NOT Bonsai's actual LoRA delta (which we
  don't have). The actual mapping might be more efficient at deep
  MLP than our simulation suggests.
- Real SGD-alpha doesn't operate on a Gaussian-noised teacher; it
  conditions on calibration activations. The "compute budget"
  prediction is approximate.
