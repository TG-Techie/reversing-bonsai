## RETRACTED — MLP-SGD-concentration claim was a sigma-misspecification artifact

> **WARNING (added after independent verification).** The original
> reading of this experiment — "SGD-alpha does most of the work at
> deep MLP gate/up" — is NOT supported. The 5% LoRA-only match rate
> at L18 mlp.gate was an artifact of using `sigma = 1.25 ×
> teacher_mean(|w|)` (the 8B q_proj ratio) uniformly across all
> tensors, when the actual `s_bonsai / teacher_mean(|w|)` ratio at
> L18 mlp.gate is ~1.96. With the correct per-tensor sigma, the
> match rate jumps to 57.6%. The experiment confounds "wrong
> sigma" with "SGD work needed", and cannot be used to infer where
> SGD compute concentrates.

## Original setup (as run)

Per-row LoRA-vs-Bonsai work-concentration test, repeated at MLP gate
/ up across depths at 8B. LoRA simulation used sigma = 1.25 *
teacher_mean uniformly (the typical ratio inferred from the q_proj
L0 fit at 8B).

```
tensor              overall match    rows >60%   rows <20%   col CV
L0  mlp.gate         0.4620            29%        13%         0.027
L18 mlp.gate         0.0503             2%        93%         0.077
L35 mlp.gate         0.1372            10%        77%         0.029
L35 mlp.up           0.2175            17%        65%         0.020
```

## Why the original interpretation was wrong

The verifier sub-agent's measurement:

```
At L18 mlp.gate, per-block s_bonsai / teacher_block_mean ratio:
  median 1.96, CV 0.16
  -> Bonsai's scales sit near 2x teacher mean at this tensor

Sigma sweep at this tensor:
  sigma_mult = 0.5  -> 0.5% match
  sigma_mult = 1.0  -> 1.8% match
  sigma_mult = 1.25 -> 5.0% match    <- what was used
  sigma_mult = 2.0  -> 57.6% match   <- right sigma for this tensor
```

So the 5% match figure isn't "SGD does the work"; it's "the
simulator was given the wrong sigma for this tensor". With per-
tensor-fitted sigma, the LoRA-only simulation matches >50% of
blocks at deep MLP gate, comparable to attention.

## What can be said (force-by-data, no inferential leap)

- Per-tensor `s_bonsai / teacher_block_mean(|w|)` ratio varies by
  tensor type and depth. The 33_* finding noted v_proj has lower
  ratio (0.87×) and most projections are ~1.1-1.24× at L0; this
  experiment surfaces that L18 mlp.gate has a higher ratio (~1.96).
- The Gaussian-noise simulation must use per-tensor-calibrated sigma
  to be useful as a discriminator.

## What CANNOT be said (the retracted claim)

"The SGD-alpha step's work is concentrated at deep MLP gate/up"
is NOT supported by this experiment. The byte signatures it claimed
to be evidence for (5% match etc.) are explained by the simulation
parameter choice, not by anything intrinsic to the technique.

## What we'd need for a correct version of this test

- Per-(layer, tensor-type) sigma calibrated to bonsai/teacher block-
  mean ratio at THAT tensor, not a uniform sigma.
- Then the residual match-rate would actually measure how much
  per-block scale variation in Bonsai is NOT captured by an
  appropriately-scaled LoRA. That residual is what SGD would need
  to fit.
- Until that recalibrated test is run, we cannot make claims about
  where SGD does its work.

The cross-tensor `27_*` finding ("per-tensor amplification factor
near-constant within a tensor at typical CV 7-40%") is the right
calibration anchor: each tensor has its own characteristic
amplification factor; a Gaussian-noise simulator should match
it per-tensor, not use a uniform value.
