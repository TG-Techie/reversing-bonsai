## Depth-resolved SVD on (W_bonsai - W_teacher) at MLP gate/up

`38_*` showed block-level flip coupling grows with depth at MLP
gate/up (over-dispersion ratio ~1.1 at L0 → ~2.2 at L35). Two
candidate mechanisms produce that pattern: **rank-grows-with-depth
LoRA** (deeper = higher LoRA rank, more block-correlated structure)
or **OBC-style sequential** (late layers compound earlier-layer
reconstruction error, produce more block-coherent per-block fits).

The clean discriminator: SVD the per-layer delta. If LoRA rank grows
with depth, rank-128 % of squared-Frobenius-norm should rise with L.
If OBC-style, rank concentration stays roughly flat (each layer's
delta is approximately full-rank by construction; the coupling lives
in *which positions flip*, not in the singular-value distribution).

## Method

Full SVD of `(W_bonsai - W_teacher)` at 5 depths × {gate, up}.
Report % of squared-Frobenius-norm captured at ranks 16, 64, 128,
256, 512.

## Reading

Rank-128 % is **essentially flat across depth at MLP**:
- ffn_gate: 7.80% (L0) → 7.66% (L9) → 7.84% (L18) → 7.96% (L27) → 8.78% (L35)
- ffn_up: 7.51% (L0) → 7.45% (L9), L18+ pending

Total spread across 5 depths at gate: 7.66-8.78% (range = 1.1pp).
The Frobenius norm of the delta DOES grow with depth (gate L0 = 192,
L35 = 287, +50%) but that growth is matched proportionally across
all singular values, not concentrated in low-rank components.

## Implication: rank-grows-with-depth LoRA is RULED OUT

If a depth-graded LoRA preprocess explained the depth-growing block
over-dispersion, rank-128 % would rise meaningfully with depth (a
LoRA rank of, say, 256 at L35 vs 64 at L0 would give rank-128
fractions 90% vs 25%). We observe ~8% throughout. The depth-growing
block coupling cannot be the LoRA component growing; the technique's
LoRA-equivalent component (if any) has uniform-rank effective
structure across depth.

## What this leaves consistent

The depth-growing block coupling at MLP must come from a mechanism
that:
- Produces block-coherent flip patterns even though the *delta* is
  approximately full-rank.
- Has its block-coupling strength grow with depth.

Mechanism families that fit:

1. **OBC-style sequential per-block quantisation**. Each block's
   sign decisions are made jointly to minimise activation
   reconstruction error, with errors compounding layer-by-layer. The
   per-block delta need not be low-rank, but the *position of
   flips within a block* is correlated because the optimiser
   couples them by construction. Late layers see more compounded
   error and produce more block-coherent fits.
2. **A depth-graded loss function** that explicitly rewards
   block-coherent flip patterns more strongly at deep layers.
   Possible but ad-hoc; the whitepaper's "principled framework"
   wording argues against this.
3. **A LoRA preprocess + per-element SGD step where the SGD's
   effective per-block "update temperature" decreases with depth**.
   Could produce growing coupling if the SGD step at deep layers
   does fewer per-block updates, leaving the LoRA's natural
   block-structure visible. Requires the LoRA to have block-
   coherent structure (rank << 128). Not directly tested.

The strongest natural fit is OBC-style sequential. It produces
exactly the conjunction observed:
- approximately full-rank delta everywhere (per-element decisions)
- block-correlated flips (per-block joint optimisation)
- depth-growing coupling (compounding earlier-layer error)

## Refinement to the recipe

A reproduction targeting Bonsai's MLP fingerprint cannot use a
"LoRA + per-element SGD" recipe with a single uniform LoRA rank;
that recipe produces uniform over-dispersion across depth. The
recipe must include EITHER:
- A per-layer optimisation step that becomes more block-coherent
  at deep layers (sequential / OBC-style), OR
- A depth-conditioned regulariser that increases block-coupling
  strength layer-by-layer.

This matches the Hassibi-group ℓ∞ paper's positioning: layer-by-layer
optimisation with activation-aware per-block decisions, not a single
global gradient pass over all weights.

## Caveats

- Tested at 5 depths × 2 MLP types = 10 SVDs. Attention types not
  re-tested at this depth; the existing 34_* spot checks at q L0,
  q L18, v L0 give rank-128 in 13.8-24.5% range — slightly more
  concentrated than MLP, but no per-depth-attention sweep done.
- Full SVD is exact; no rank-truncation noise. The flatness is
  not an artifact of approximate SVD.
- Bonsai's d_intermediate at 8B is 12288 (compared to d_model 4096),
  so the gate/up matrices are taller-than-wide. Same SVD pattern
  expected to hold for down_proj (transposed shape).
