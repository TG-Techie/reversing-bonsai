## Per-layer scale-CV vs sign-match correlation at 8B

For each (layer, projection_type) at 8B, compute:
  - cv = per-block scale std / mean  (from GGUF, no dequant)
  - sign-match = fraction of nonzero positions matching teacher sign
                 (from reports/local-8B/20_full_36layer_audit.txt)

Then correlate per-layer (cv, sign-match) for each projection type
across the 36 layers.

If cv and sign-match track the same underlying disturbance, the
correlation should be NEGATIVE (high cv = low sign-match). If they
are independent signals, correlation ~0.

```
type    n    pearson(cv, sign-match)
----    --   ----------------------
gate    36     -0.69
up      36     -0.90    ← strongest signal
down    36     -0.31
q       36     -0.48
k       36     -0.23
v       36     -0.49
o       36     +0.17    ← anomalous
```

## Reading

Six of seven projection types show NEGATIVE correlation: per-block
scale variability and sign-disagreement-with-teacher track each other
across depth. Layers that are "disturbed" (low sign-match-vs-base)
also show wide per-block scale distributions; layers that are "near
teacher" (high sign-match) show tight scale distributions. Same
underlying disturbance signal, different metric.

`up_proj` is the strongest example: Pearson -0.90 across 36 layers
between per-block scale-CV and sign-match. The L1-3 MLP-up dip in
sign-match (0.62, 0.63, 0.66) coincides with high CVs at those layers
(0.51, 0.53, 0.40). Same with `gate_proj` (-0.69) and the q/v
attention projections (-0.48 / -0.49).

`o_proj` is the lone exception (+0.17). Possible structural reason:
o_proj's rows index hidden-dim (not head-dim like q/k/v), so its
per-block scale distribution sits in a different space than the
other three attention projections. The technique's disturbance
signal apparently doesn't show up in o_proj scale-CV the way it does
in q/k/v.

## Recipe constraint

A reproduction targeting Bonsai-style behaviour can use either
metric — per-block scale-CV OR sign-match-vs-teacher — to predict
which layers received the strongest disturbance. The two metrics
collapse onto a single per-layer "disturbance" axis for MLP and for
q/k/v. For o_proj specifically, a separate metric is needed.

This finding aligns with the v3 recipe's step-4: a per-layer SGD-α
step that flips signs and chooses scales jointly. Under that recipe,
both signs and scales are outputs of the same optimisation, so they
naturally co-vary by layer.
