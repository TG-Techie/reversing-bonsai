## Per-row delta concentration at 8B q_proj L0

For each row of the (4096, 4096) tensor, compute:
- `delta_row_norm[i]`     = L2 norm of (W_bonsai[i] - W_teacher[i])
- `teacher_row_norm[i]`   = L2 norm of W_teacher[i]
- `bonsai_row_mean[i]`    = mean(|W_bonsai[i]|)

Correlate across rows.

```
Per-row delta-norm vs teacher-row-norm:
  Pearson  +0.873
  Spearman +0.864
  -> big-norm rows have big-delta-norm (proportional)

Per-row delta-norm vs per-row mean(deployed Bonsai):
  Pearson  +0.948   <-- stronger; deployed-Bonsai magnitude tracks delta most
  Spearman +0.944
```

Cumulative squared-norm by ranked rows:

```
top  1% of rows: 1.9% of delta squared-norm
top  5%       : 8.2%
top 10%       : 15.7%
top 25%       : 37.2%
top 50%       : 67.8%

uniform expectation: top X% holds X% of squared-norm
```

## Reading

The delta is MODESTLY row-concentrated. Top 50% of rows hold 67.8%
of squared-norm vs the uniform expectation of 50% — concentration
ratio ~1.36x.

This is consistent with the per-row amplification story (24_*, 26_*):
the technique perturbs each row roughly proportionally to its
teacher amplitude. Big-norm rows get bigger absolute delta because
they have more magnitude to perturb. Not extreme outlier-row
concentration.

The Pearson 0.87 (delta vs teacher) and 0.95 (delta vs Bonsai)
together say:

1. Delta tracks teacher row-norm closely.
2. Delta tracks Bonsai's per-row mean even more closely (since
   Bonsai's amplification factor is roughly ~2x teacher per
   tensor, and that factor is multiplicative on row magnitudes).
3. The technique inherits per-row magnitude structure from
   teacher; doesn't pick out a small set of "outlier rows" to
   amplify disproportionately.

## Recipe constraint

A reproduction's delta should:

- Have per-row L2-norm correlate with teacher-row-norm at Pearson
  ~0.85+.
- Be modestly row-concentrated (top 50% rows ~65-70% of squared-
  norm, not 90%+).
- NOT exhibit a small-outlier-row pattern (the per-row amplification
  is roughly proportional to teacher row magnitude, not selectively
  applied to a salient subset).

## Caveats

- One tensor at one size. Different tensor types may have different
  concentration profiles. MLP gate/up's per-row Pearson with teacher
  is lower (~0.6 early, ~0.2 late per 26_*), so the delta there
  may be MORE row-concentrated.
