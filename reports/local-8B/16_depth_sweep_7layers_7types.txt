## Patterns

- Attention projections (q/k/v/o): joint r² mostly 0.45-0.80 across
  depth. Stable — base block statistics keep predicting much of
  Bonsai's chosen scales for attention paths.

- MLP (gate / up / down): wildly non-monotonic. L6 mlp_gate r² =
  0.016 (essentially noise). L35 mlp_up r² = 0.048 (essentially
  noise). But L30 mlp_down r² = 0.861, and L0 mlp_down r² = 0.818
  (highly predictable). The "predictability collapse" is not
  smooth in depth.

## Hypothesis fit

This pattern fits naturally with a forward-sequential quantization
where each layer's scale-choice depends on accumulated activation
distortion from prior layers (GPTQ-style or similar). Under that
hypothesis:

  - r² is base-block-driven where activations through to that layer
    happen to remain correlated with teacher activations.
  - r² collapses where prior-layer quantization has decorrelated the
    activations from teacher activations — and the new optimization
    target is the distorted activation distribution, not the teacher
    weights.

That predicts the erratic, non-monotonic depth pattern we observe in
MLP, which a clean QAT story would not produce.

A reproduction recipe consistent with this would:
  1. Quantize embedding via the formula directly (force-by-data).
  2. Sweep through layers in topological order, at each layer:
     - Solve for sign and scale jointly under an objective tied to
       the activation through the partially-quantized model up to
       this point.
     - Bias scales upward to compensate for downstream activation
       attenuation.
  3. Allow specific norms (input_layernorm, post_attn_layernorm) to
     update as part of this; freeze q_norm/k_norm.
  4. Optionally interleave or follow with an instruction-tune for
     identity / behavioural shaping.
