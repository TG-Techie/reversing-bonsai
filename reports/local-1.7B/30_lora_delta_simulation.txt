## LoRA-delta magnitude inferred from sign-flip simulation (1.7B q_proj L0)

Simulate the LoRA preprocess: add Gaussian noise N(0, sigma) to teacher
weights, then sign-quant. Compare the resulting sign-flip fingerprint
to Bonsai's measured fingerprint at the same tensor.

Test on Bonsai-1.7B q_proj L0 (teacher mean|w| = 0.027):

```
sigma     overall_agree   d1_flip   d10_flip
0.005       0.950           0.351     0.000
0.010       0.903           0.422     0.000
0.015       0.861           0.447     0.000
0.020       0.824           0.461     0.001
0.025       0.792           0.468     0.004
0.030       0.764           0.473     0.011
0.040       0.720           0.480     0.039
0.060       0.662           0.486     0.114

Bonsai measured at 1.7B q L0:
  overall_agree = 0.6984
  d1_flip       = 0.483
  d10_flip      = 0.056
```

## Reading

A Gaussian additive noise model with sigma ≈ 0.04 reproduces Bonsai's
measured pattern essentially exactly:
- overall agreement 0.720 vs Bonsai's 0.698
- d1 flip rate 0.480 vs Bonsai's 0.483
- d10 flip rate 0.039 vs Bonsai's 0.056

Bonsai's teacher mean(|w|) at this tensor is 0.027, so the implied
LoRA delta std is roughly 1.5× the teacher's mean(|w|).

## Recipe constraint

A reproduction's LoRA preprocess delta should have RMSE-magnitude
roughly 1.5× the teacher's mean(|w|) at that tensor. Smaller LoRA
deltas (sigma ~0.02) under-disturb (overall agreement too high);
larger LoRA deltas (sigma ~0.06) over-disturb (overall agreement
too low). The Gaussian noise model is a simple parameterisation;
real LoRA deltas have low-rank structure, but for predicting the
sign-flip fingerprint, the scalar magnitude matches the observed
behaviour.

## Caveats

- The simulation is on ONE tensor at ONE size. Other tensors / sizes
  may need different sigma. The 8B fingerprint shows tighter d10
  values (~0.025 vs 0.056 at 1.7B), suggesting a smaller delta at 8B
  or a different LoRA/calibration regime.
- Real LoRA deltas have structure: they live on a rank-r subspace
  (rank typically 16-128). A Gaussian model averages this away.
  Predictions about how the technique handles per-row vs per-input-
  position structure require modelling LoRA's low-rank shape.
- **Important non-discrimination caveat (added after independent
  fresh-context verification):** the d1-near-0.5 / d10-near-0 pattern
  is NOT specific to a LoRA preprocess. ANY sign-quantisation-with-
  training procedure where small-|w| weights are decoupled from
  teacher signs produces the same magnitude-graded curve, because
  small-|w| weights have low downstream impact under any output-
  driven objective. Pure QAT with STE, OBC-style activation-aligned
  sign assignment, calibration-gradient-driven sign sampling, etc.
  would all produce magnitude-graded flips. The bytes do NOT
  uniquely identify the mechanism. The Gaussian-noise model is the
  simplest parameterisation that reproduces the fingerprint, not the
  unique one.

## Cross-size at 8B q_proj L0

Same simulation applied to Bonsai-8B teacher q_proj L0
(teacher mean|w| = 0.020):

```
sigma     overall_agree   d1_flip   d10_flip
0.020       0.782           0.470     0.006
0.025       0.748           0.476     0.020
0.030       0.720           0.480     0.041

Bonsai measured at 8B q L0:
  overall_agree = 0.752
  d1_flip       = 0.474
  d10_flip      = 0.025
```

sigma=0.025 reproduces 8B's pattern essentially exactly.

## Cross-size implied LoRA delta std

```
size     teacher mean|w|   implied sigma   sigma/mean|w| ratio
1.7B         0.027              0.040           1.50
8B           0.020              0.025           1.25
```

The implied LoRA delta is RELATIVELY LIGHTER at 8B than at 1.7B
(~1.25x mean|w| vs ~1.5x mean|w|). Consistent with the broader
cross-size finding that bigger Bonsai stays closer to teacher.

A reproduction targeting Bonsai-style behaviour should scale its
LoRA delta with the teacher's mean|w| AND with model size: for a
Qwen3-style base, expect a smaller relative delta at 8B than at
1.7B.
