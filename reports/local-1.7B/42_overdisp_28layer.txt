## Cross-size validation: 1.7B 28-layer over-dispersion sweep

`40_*` at 8B revealed a striking L1-L3 MLP "disturbance spike" with
over-dispersion 10-13. This experiment runs the same sweep at 1.7B
(28 layers) to test whether the spike is a **universal recipe-step
signature** (and so should appear at every size) or an
**8B-specific feature** of the algorithm's interaction with that
particular teacher.

## Method

Identical to `local-8B/40_*`'s `depth_overdisp_sweep.py`, retargeted
at the 1.7B GGUF + base.

## Cross-size comparison: where 1.7B differs from 8B

### The L1-L3 MLP "disturbance spike" is 8B-specific

```
location          8B over-disp       1.7B over-disp
L1 ffn_gate            10.27              1.57
L1 ffn_up              11.06              2.47
L2 ffn_gate            10.20              1.38
L2 ffn_up              13.03              1.15
L3 ffn_gate            12.11              1.29
L3 ffn_up              10.37              1.17
```

At 1.7B the L1 over-dispersion is 1.6-2.5 (modestly elevated) — far
from the 10-13 of 8B. The "spike" is NOT universal. **A separate
L1-3 rewrite step in the recipe (uniformly applied across sizes) is
RULED OUT** — it would have produced the same spike at 1.7B.

### Patterns that DO replicate cross-size

```
pattern                                 8B         1.7B
attn_q over-dispersion range          1.7-3.2     1.8-3.8
attn_v rises monotonically            1.1-1.8     1.0-1.8
deep-MLP rise at last layer           gate 2.16   gate 2.31
                                       up 2.25     up 1.66
ffn_down stays modest (1.0-1.7)       yes         yes
mid-layer middle plateau              yes         yes
```

The qualitative shape — q uniformly elevated, v rising with depth,
deep-MLP rise at end, mid-layer plateau, modest down — is preserved.
What's NOT preserved: the L1-3 spike's MAGNITUDE (and to a lesser
extent, location).

### Quantitative cross-size offsets

- 1.7B has higher baseline flip rates (~0.28-0.32 vs 8B's 0.20-0.30).
  Consistent with the "smaller Bonsai = more deviation from teacher"
  finding from `local-8B/26_*` and earlier.
- 1.7B has higher v over-dispersion baseline (1.69 at L0 vs 8B's
  1.12 at L0).
- 1.7B's down has a mid-layer hump (~1.4-1.7 around L8-25) that's
  modest at 8B.

## Implication for the recipe

Three readings of the 8B-only L1-3 spike are consistent with the
cross-size data:

1. **Pure-OBC-style sequential applied identically across sizes**.
   The L1-3 spike is an 8B-specific *output* of the algorithm
   meeting Qwen3-8B's specific weight distribution. At 1.7B the same
   algorithm produces only modest L1 elevation. No special-case step
   needed. **This fits PrismML's "principled framework, not narrow
   exceptions" framing**.

2. **Single algorithm with size-conditioned parameters**. The
   recipe has hyperparameters (e.g., per-layer regularisation
   strength, OBC step size) that PrismML tuned per-size. At 8B the
   tuning happened to make L1-3 highly block-coherent; at 1.7B the
   same algorithm with different hyperparameters produced a flatter
   profile.

3. **Two distinct recipes**. PrismML used different
   approaches at different sizes. Lowest probability given the
   whitepaper's unified framework claim.

The bytes don't discriminate (1) from (2). They DO rule out reading
(3) plus any hypothesis requiring an explicit per-layer-targeted
step at L1-3.

## Recipe constraint refinement

A reproduction at 8B should produce the L1-3 spike *as a side effect*
of the algorithm running on Qwen3-8B's teacher weights — not as the
result of an explicit L1-3-targeted step. If a recipe produces the
spike at 1.7B too (where Bonsai-1.7B doesn't have one), the recipe
has the wrong mechanism even if it matches 8B numbers.

The size-specific spike is a **discriminator** between mechanism
families:
- Algorithms with size-invariant per-layer treatment (e.g., a
  constant LoRA rank applied to all layers): would produce the spike
  at every size. **Doesn't match.**
- Algorithms with size-conditioned compounding (OBC-style with the
  early-layer error compounding more strongly when more layers
  follow it): could produce the spike only at 8B. **Matches.**

## Caveats

- Single-seed measurements at each size; no resampling-noise
  estimate.
- 1.7B has 28 layers vs 8B's 36. The "depth fraction" comparison
  may be more meaningful than raw layer-index. Eg L1/28 = 3.6% of
  depth at 1.7B; L1/36 = 2.8% at 8B; close enough.
- 4B (32 layers) is not re-measured here. The 4B trio fits in the
  download budget if needed for a third cross-size point.
