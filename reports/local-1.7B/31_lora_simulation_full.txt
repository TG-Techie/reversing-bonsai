## Full-recipe step-1+step-2 simulation vs Bonsai bytes (1.7B q_proj L0)

The candidate recipe v4 has two steps before SGD-alpha refinement:
1. LoRA preprocess: w_LoRA = w_teacher + small_delta
2. Naive Q1_0: formula(w_LoRA) = sign(w_LoRA) * mean(|w_LoRA|_per_128_block)

Test: simulate steps 1+2 with a Gaussian delta and compare to Bonsai bytes.

```
                                                  sign-match   byte-match   per-block scale
                                                  to Bonsai    (abs 1e-3)   match (within 10%)
formula(raw teacher) [no LoRA]                    0.6984       0.0155       n/a
formula(teacher + N(0, 0.020))                    0.6674       0.0425       0.221
formula(teacher + N(0, 0.025))                    0.6558       0.0634       0.327
formula(teacher + N(0, 0.030))                    0.6445       0.0738       0.414
formula(teacher + N(0, 0.035))                    0.6342       0.0741       0.435    <-- best per-block
formula(teacher + N(0, 0.040))                    0.6245       0.0619       0.382
formula(teacher + N(0, 0.050))                    0.6082       0.0225       0.178
```

## Reading

`formula(raw teacher)` already matches Bonsai's signs at 0.7 — same as
the audit's measured overall sign-agreement. So Bonsai's signs are
about 70% the same as what `formula(raw teacher)` would produce.

`formula(teacher + Gaussian noise)` gives LOWER sign-match-to-Bonsai
than `formula(raw teacher)`. This is because the simulation flips
DIFFERENT positions than Bonsai actually flipped. Both have the
SAME RATE of flips (matched by the 30_* magnitude-graded test) but
the SPECIFIC positions flipped depend on Bonsai's actual calibration
data + LoRA training trajectory, which a random Gaussian delta does
not reproduce.

## What the LoRA-preprocess does NOT do

- It does NOT pin the specific positions that flip. Different
  calibration data / LoRA initialisations would flip different
  positions. Only the magnitude-graded distribution of flips is
  reproducible.

## What the LoRA-preprocess DOES do (per this test)

- Matches Bonsai's per-block scales for ~43% of blocks (within 10%).
  This is significant given the formula(raw teacher) byte-match is
  only 1.5%.

## The 57% gap

The remaining 57% of blocks need scale corrections beyond what
LoRA + naive Q1_0 produces. This is consistent with the v4 recipe
including a per-block SGD-alpha step (step 3) that fits scales to
calibration activations rather than to weight statistics. The
SGD-alpha step accounts for the gap between LoRA-only-scale-match
(43%) and Bonsai's actual scales.

## Recipe-implication update

Steps 1 + 2 alone:
- match the d1/d10 magnitude-graded sign-flip fingerprint
- match ~43% of per-block scales
- DO NOT match Bonsai's specific position-of-flip choices

Step 3 (SGD-alpha):
- account for the remaining ~57% of per-block scales
- driven by calibration-activation-output objective, not weight-distance

Steps 4-7: layer-by-layer ordering, lm_head, norms, identity-shaping.

## Caveats

- The test is on ONE tensor. The 57% gap may be tighter or wider on
  other tensors.
- Real LoRA delta is low-rank, not Gaussian. A low-rank delta would
  affect the SAME positions consistently across rows (since the
  delta has structure). Could potentially RAISE the sign-match-to-
  Bonsai if the LoRA happens to flip positions the technique
  expected to flip.
- 8B per-tensor scale-match might be higher (8B has tighter d10
  values, so the LoRA delta is smaller, so the residual SGD-alpha
  step has less to correct).
